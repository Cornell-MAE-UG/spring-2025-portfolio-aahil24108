# Fluid Mechanical Dissection

## Project Overview

For our Fluid Mechanics group project in MAE 3230, we completed a fluid mechanical dissection of a pump system. The goal of this project was to analyze how fluid mechanics principles appear in a real-world engineering system and explain the physical behavior behind it.

Our group focused on understanding how fluid moves through the pump, how pressure and velocity change throughout the system, and how energy is transferred to the fluid. Through this project, we connected class topics such as conservation of mass, conservation of momentum, pressure forces, viscosity, and energy losses to a practical mechanical engineering application.

## Engineering Analysis

In this project, we examined how fluid motion affects the performance and behavior of a pump. We considered how the fluid enters the system, moves through the pump, and exits with changes in pressure, velocity, and energy.

Some of the main fluid mechanics concepts we applied included:

- Flow rate and continuity
- Pressure differences
- Energy transfer
- Head loss
- Viscous effects
- Momentum transfer
- Real-world assumptions and simplifications

By analyzing these ideas, we were able to better understand how theoretical fluid mechanics principles can be used to explain an actual engineering system.
